Criminal Justice survey for decision-support system comparison

Motivation

Automatic decision-support systems are utilized by judges in the US to decide whether to grant bail to defendants charged with non-violent crimes. These automatic decision-support systems use machine learning (ML) model to make the decision, in this experiment, you will see two options as two different decisions made by two different decision-support systems. We would like you to give your honest opinion about which decision do you think is fairer.

After you complete this task, you will help to improve the machine learning in the decision-support system to be fairer.

Detailed Instructions

Determine which option is fairer

  1. Begin by carefully fill in the participant's information
  2. Next scroll down, look at the example presented and determine if Option A or Option B is fairer
  3. Depends on the example given, select the appropriate category: (1) Option A is much fairer, (2) Option A is fairer, (3) Don't know, (4) Option B is fairer or (5) Option B is much fairer

Example explanation

The top chart represents the defendants and their race or nationality. Then in the table below, the first row is the true outcomes of these defendants, whether they did reoffend or not. If a criminal reoffends, it means that they commit more crime after having been punished for a previous crime. The second row and the third row are the two options A and B predicted by two different decision-support systems using ML models. You should select an option on the left side to decide which option you think is fairer.
